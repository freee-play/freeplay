// REASONS TO SKIP ►
01
😴
// Sleep Science
Sleep Deprivation Tanks Performance
Studies show that attending class on under 5 hours of sleep actually results in worse retention than not attending at all. Sometimes staying home and sleeping IS the academically correct move.
02
🧠
// Mental Health
Burnout is a Real Academic Risk
Chronic academic stress without rest leads to burnout — which can cause GPA to crater far more than a single absence ever would. Strategic recovery days are self-regulation, not laziness.
03
📡
// Information Access
Most Content Exists Online Already
For standard lectures, Khan Academy, YouTube, and the textbook often teach the same material with better pacing and the ability to pause and rewind. Not all classes add value over self-study.
04
🎯
// Opportunity Cost
Some Opportunities Don't Repeat
A job interview, a once-in-a-semester networking event, or a mental health crisis — there are legitimate reasons to prioritize other things. One class is almost never worth missing a real opportunity.
05
⚡
// Productivity
Deep Work Beats Passive Sitting
An hour of focused solo study in the right environment often produces better results than a 90-minute lecture you're not engaged in. Know when you'll absorb more on your own.
06
🎰
// Risk Analysis
Most Policies Allow Some Absences
The majority of syllabi allow 2–3 absences without penalty. Those are built in deliberately. Using one strategically — for a day you genuinely need — is exactly what they're designed for.
07
🌐
// The Real World
Self-Management is the Meta-Skill
Knowing when to push through and when to recover is a life skill more valuable than any single lecture. The ability to self-regulate, assess priorities, and make judgment calls — that's what employers actually hire for.
08
🎭
// Honesty
Sometimes You Just Need a Day
Not everything needs a justification. Rest is a human need. If you've been consistent, completed your work, and you need a reset day — that's a reasonable human decision. Don't spiral about it.
09
⚖️
// The Verdict
But Don't Make It a Habit
This page exists as a counterpoint — not permission to ghost your entire semester. The data from the other page is real too. Use your skips wisely. Make them count.
