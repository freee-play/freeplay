🌍 Topics We Love

Totally Random, Totally Awesome

We cover everything that makes the world interesting — from the bottom of the ocean to the edge of the galaxy. None of it is math. All of it is incredible.

🦕

Dinosaurs

The T-Rex had arms so short it could not clap. Scientists believe this is why they went extinct — they couldn't applaud each other's achievements and simply lost all motivation.

🚀

Outer Space

Space is completely silent because there is no air for sound to travel through. This means no one in space can hear you sing, which is either great news or terrible news depending on your voice.

🌋

Volcanoes

A volcano is essentially the Earth clearing its throat. It has been doing this for about 4.5 billion years and shows no signs of stopping. The Earth is extremely congested.

🐙

Deep Sea Creatures

The anglerfish has a glowing light on its head to lure prey in the pitch-dark ocean. Scientists who study it are still unsure whether the anglerfish knows how cool it looks.

🌩️

Weather & Storms

Lightning strikes the Earth about 100 times every single second. None of those strikes have ever apologized, which is considered extremely rude by the global meteorology community.

🦜

Weird Animals

The platypus is a mammal that lays eggs, has a duck bill, a beaver tail, and detects electric fields. Scientists described it as "nature not reading the rules." They were entirely correct.

🤯 Absolutely True Fact

Cleopatra lived closer in time to the Moon landing than to the construction of the Great Pyramid of Giza. The pyramids were so ancient that Cleopatra herself was basically a modern tourist visiting old ruins.
